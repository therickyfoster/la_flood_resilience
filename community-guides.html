Evacuation Go-Bag Checklist

Prepare these items in advance. Keep bag near door for quick departure:

  • Water & Food1 gallon per person per day (3-day supply), non-perishable snacks
  • Medications7-day supply of prescriptions, first aid kit
  • DocumentsID, insurance papers, birth certificates (copies in waterproof bag)
  • Phone & ChargersBattery bank, car charger, emergency radio
  • CashSmall bills, ATMs may be down during outages
  • Flashlight & BatteriesLED flashlight, extra batteries
  • Change of ClothesWarm layers, rain jacket, sturdy shoes
  • Pet SuppliesFood, water, carrier, medications, vaccination records

Home Flood-Proofing Tips

üö™ Door & Window Barriers

Stack sandbags in pyramid formation (3-2-1) at doorways. Overlap bags like bricks. Don't stack higher than 2 feet without wider base.

üåßÔ∏è Clear Gutters & Drains

Remove leaves and debris from gutters, downspouts, and yard drains. Ensure water flows away from foundation.

‚ö° Protect Electrical

Move electrical items above potential flood level. Know how to shut off main breaker. Never touch electrical in flooded areas.

üöó Move Vehicles

Park cars on higher ground before storm. Never drive through flooded roads‚Äî"Turn Around, Don't Drown."

üì∏ Document Property

Photograph belongings before storm for insurance. Store photos in cloud. Keep insurance policy info accessible.

üîå Fill Bathtubs

If water service may be disrupted, fill bathtubs and containers with clean water for drinking and sanitation.

Resources for Unhoused Individuals

LAHSA and partner agencies provide storm-specific support:

  • Outreach Teams: Deployed 48 hours before storms to warn and offer services
  • Hotel Vouchers: Activated during severe weather events
  • Winter Shelter Program: Additional beds available Nov-March
  • Call 211: 24/7 helpline for shelter referrals

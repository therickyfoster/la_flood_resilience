Debris Flow Mitigation Protocols

Specialized protocols for post-fire debris flow prevention including basin management, slope stabilization, and early warning systems.

Critical Understanding Debris flows are NOT mudslides. They're "floods on steroids" (USGS) moving at highway speeds with the consistency of wet concrete. Recent burn scars remain vulnerable for 2-5 years post-fire.

‚ö†Ô∏è Early Warning Response Protocol

When NWS issues debris flow warnings for burn scar areas:

48 Hours Before Storm

Evacuation warnings issued. LAPD/LASD conduct door-to-door notifications at high-risk properties. Residents should prepare go-bags and review evacuation routes.

24 Hours Before Storm

Evacuation orders may be issued for specific properties. K-rail and barrier positions verified. Swift water rescue teams pre-staged. Road closure equipment positioned.

During Storm

Real-time rain gauge monitoring. Flash Flood Warnings issued when thresholds approached. Road closures activated. Emergency response teams deployed to access points.

Post-Storm

Damage assessment teams deployed. Debris basin capacity checked. Road clearing operations begin. Evacuation orders lifted only after inspection.

‚õ∞Ô∏è Slope Stabilization Techniques

Cal OES Watershed Task Force has installed 630,250+ feet of protection materials:

  • Straw Wattles: Tubes placed along slope contours to slow runoff
  • Silt Fencing: Filter barriers to capture sediment
  • Compost Socks: Organic filters for contaminated runoff
  • Plastic Sheeting: Hillside tarps to reduce erosion (777 rolls stockpiled)
  • Hydromulch: Spray-applied seed/mulch mixture for revegetation
